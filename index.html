About Me

Prior to studying software, I started my professional career as a team lead in construction. As a team lead, it was often my responsibility to conduct hiring trials and train new team members. What made a candidate stand out to me in these trials, and going forward as a member of our team, was not the specialized knowledge they already had to their advantage, but their genuine willingness and passion towards learning and problem-solving. As I transition from building homes to building software, I believe that this attitude is my most valuable asset.

I've spent the last several months practicing critical thinking with a software focus, learning new libraries and programming principles along the way. In that same time I've deployed a few web applications using React.js with Redux for the front-end, and an Express.js or Flask server for the back-end. I used SCRUM methodologies to manage project timelines, and Git for version control. Head straight over to my GitHub or continue scrolling to check out these projects!

Recent Projects

Gloom

Gloom is a full stack note-taking web app inspired by Evernote.com. Registered users are able to make notebooks and add individual pages to those notebooks. Notebook pages support rich-text with the app's integrated rich-text editor. The user's personal dashboard provides quick access to their recently created pages, bookmarked pages, and scratchpad. Gloom is built on React.js and Flask.

TbdBnB

TbdBnB is a full stack homestay marketplace site (clone of AirBnB.com). Users are able to browse posted spots and their details. Registered users are able to book specific dates for spots, or host their own spots. TbdBnB is built on React.js and Express.js.

Scuttlr

Scuttlr is a multimedia blogging site (clone of Tumblr.com). This site was a collaboration between myself, Nate Lumpkin, Brad Lewter, and David Siwulec. Users are able to browse a feed of all posts on Scuttlr. Registered users are able to follow specific users, and view a filtered feed. Registered users are also able to like and comment on specific posts. Scuttlr is built on React.js and Flask.
